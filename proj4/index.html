Overview

In this project, I implement a real-time simulation of cloth using a mass and spring based system. Features included: applying physical constraints on the mass and spring based system, applying numerical integration to simulate the way cloth moves over time, implementing collisions with other objects, implementing self-collisions to prevent cloth clipping, implementing shaders.

Part 5: Shaders

Explain in your own words what is a shader program and how vertex and fragment shaders work together to create lighting and material effects.

A shader program is a program that runs in the graphic pipeline and tells the computer how to render each pixel. It is used to control lighting and shading effects. Vertex shader describe the attributes (positions, normal vector, etc) of a vertex and sends this information to fragment shader. Fragment shader takes in the information from vertex shader and renders/shades a fragment/pixel based on lightings, materials, and other conditions.

 

Explain the Blinn-Phong shading model in your own words. Show a screenshot of your Blinn-Phong shader outputting only the ambient component, a screen shot only outputting the diffuse component, a screen shot only outputting the specular component, and one using the entire Blinn-Phong model.

Blinn-Phong shading model is a combination of specular highlights, diffuse reflection, and ambient lighting. Specular highlights is highly view dependent - as you move your view position, the specular highlights on the surface move over the surface quickly. Diffuse reflection does not dependent on your view point. Ambient lighting is like a hack to make things not look totally dark in the black. For displacement mapping, when the mesh of the sphere is more coarse, the vertices are undersampling the texture. There aren't enough vertices to sample the jump in height in the texture. When the mesh of the sphere has a higher resolution, the jumps in height in texture is sampled better and we are able to see more details. For bump mapping, the differences between higher or lower resolutions spheres are much smaller compared to displacement mapping. Visually, bumping mapping seems to perform better than displacement mapping with lower sampling rate.
